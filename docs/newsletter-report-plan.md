# Purrmission Newsletter and Report Plan

## Purpose

The product MVP and the newsletter/report should run in parallel, but the newsletter should stay lightweight at first.

The product validates:

> Will users ask the cat before they buy?

The newsletter validates:

> Can Purrmission become a memorable consumer-behavior and cat-judgment content brand?

These two loops should support each other. The newsletter brings early audience and language testing. Product behavior can later become original report data.

## Positioning

The newsletter should not feel like a personal finance column.

It should feel like:

- consumer behavior observation
- shopping psychology
- cat court
- impulse confession
- practical pre-purchase challenge

Working title:

> The Wallet Court by Purrmission

Other possible titles:

- Purrmission Weekly
- The Weekly Purrmission Report
- Purrsonality Brief
- Ask the Cat Weekly

## Recommended Format

Publish once per week.

Target length: 1200 to 1800 words.

Each issue should follow a repeatable structure:

1. **This Week's Temptation**
   - One shopping trap or decision theme.

2. **Why We Fall For It**
   - A plain-language behavior explanation such as scarcity, present bias, social proof, discount framing, sunk cost, or identity shopping.

3. **Cat's Verdict**
   - A short Purrmission-style judgment with personality.

4. **What To Ask Before Buying**
   - Three to five practical questions users can ask before checkout.

5. **This Week's Challenge**
   - A simple action such as 48-Hour Paws, No Duplicate Week, Cart Graveyard, or Return Policy Check.

6. **Purrsonality Note**
   - A playful shopping personality observation.

7. **Soft CTA**
   - Join the waitlist, submit a purchase confession, ask the cat, or share with a friend.

## AI-Assisted Pipeline

Do not fully automate publishing at the start.

The right early workflow:

1. Maintain a topic backlog.
2. Pick one topic each week.
3. Use AI to generate an outline.
4. Use AI to gather research notes and source candidates.
5. Use AI to draft the issue.
6. Manually review facts, claims, tone, and cat personality.
7. Publish through Substack, beehiiv, or the website.
8. Turn the issue into short social posts.

The key rule:

> AI can draft. A human approves.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

Lightweight weekly schedule:

- Monday: pick topic and generate outline
- Tuesday: gather research notes and draft
- Wednesday: edit logic, examples, and cat voice
- Thursday: publish
- Friday: split into social posts
- Weekend: review clicks, comments, replies, and topic ideas

If time is limited:

- Wednesday night: generate draft
- Thursday night: edit
- Friday: publish

The newsletter should take 2 to 3 hours per week at first.

## Platform Recommendation

### Fastest Start

Use Substack.

Pros:

- fastest setup
- built-in email list
- easy scheduled publishing
- low engineering burden

Cons:

- less control
- weaker product data integration
- limited automation

Best for immediate audience testing.

### Growth-Oriented

Use beehiiv.

Pros:

- stronger newsletter growth tooling
- referral and monetization features
- better suited for newsletter-as-channel

Cons:

- more setup
- more platform complexity

Best if newsletter growth becomes a serious channel.

### Product-Integrated

Use a Purrmission blog plus Resend later.

Pros:

- full brand control
- can connect product emails, reports, and newsletter
- better long-term data integration

Cons:

- requires engineering
- requires unsubscribe, compliance, templates, and deliverability work

Best after the MVP and backend are stable.

Recommended sequence:

1. Start with Substack or beehiiv.
2. Mirror good issues to the Purrmission site later.
3. Move to product-integrated email only when user reports and backend events are ready.

## Data Inputs

Early content inputs:

- topic
- target audience
- consumer behavior concept
- examples
- sources
- cat persona
- challenge of the week
- CTA

Later product-data inputs:

- top categories this week
- most common risk flags
- average Purrmission score
- most Purrhibited categories
- most Pawsed categories
- listen rate
- regret rate
- top Purrsonality types
- popular counter-purrposals

The long-term goal is to evolve from public consumer-behavior commentary into Purrmission-owned shopping behavior reports.

## Monetization Role

The newsletter should not be monetized aggressively at first.

Early role:

- audience building
- waitlist growth
- voice testing
- content-market fit
- product feedback

Later revenue paths:

- sponsored issues
- premium monthly reports
- paid Purrsonality reports
- paid challenges
- conversion into AI packs or premium plans

Avoid turning it into a shopping deals newsletter. That would weaken the core promise that the cat is on the user's side.

## Priority Balance

Recommended early effort split:

- MVP product: 60%
- IP and cat language: 20%
- newsletter/report: 15%
- monetization experiments: 5%

The newsletter matters, but it should not consume product development.
